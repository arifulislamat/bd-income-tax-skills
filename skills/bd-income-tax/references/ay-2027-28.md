# AY 2027-28 — parameters (legislated, mirrors AY 2026-27)

**Income year:** 1 Jul 2026 – 30 Jun 2027 → **Assessment Year 2027-28**.
**Governing law:** Income Tax Act 2023 as amended. The general **Tk 375,000** threshold, the
**0/10/15/20/25/30%** slab schedule, and the **flat Tk 5,000** minimum tax were legislated by
the **Finance Ordinance 2025** for *both* AY 2026-27 **and** AY 2027-28 (a two-year fix),
reaffirmed in the FY2026-27 budget. `[ITA-2023]` `[FO-2025]` `[FB-2026]`

> ⚠️ These figures are **already legislated**, not guessed — but they remain **subject to
> amendment by the Finance Act 2027** (budget ~Jun 2027). Treat an AY 2027-28 result as the
> best current estimate, not a final filing figure, and say so. Confirm against the gazetted
> Finance Act 2027 and current NBR circulars before filing.

## What this year uses

AY 2027-28 **mirrors AY 2026-27** in every parameter the calculator uses — thresholds by
category, the slab schedule (5% slab abolished), the BDT 500,000 salary-exemption cap, the
Section 78 investment rebate, the flat Tk 5,000 / Tk 1,000-new-taxpayer minimum tax, and the
net-wealth surcharge bands. See **`ay-2026-27.md`** for the full tables; `tax_calc.py` builds
`PARAMS["2027-28"]` as a copy of `2026-27` so the two never drift.

Run it explicitly with `--year 2027-28`. This is the year to use for forward-looking
"what will I owe next year after my increment" questions.
