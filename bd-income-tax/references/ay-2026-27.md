# AY 2026-27 — parameters (primary year)

**Income year:** 1 Jul 2025 – 30 Jun 2026 → **Assessment Year 2026-27**.
**Governing law:** Income Tax Act 2023 as amended; rates pre-set in the Finance Ordinance
2025 and confirmed by the **Finance Bill 2026** (placed in Parliament 11 Jun 2026).
`[ITA-2023]` `[FB-2026]`

> ⚠️ As of mid-June 2026 these FY2026-27 measures are in a **Finance Bill**, expected to
> become the **Finance Act 2026** effective 1 Jul 2026. Tell users to confirm against the
> gazetted Act and current NBR circulars.

All figures are in BDT. Numbers feed `scripts/tax_calc.py` — never hand-calculate.

## General individual slab `[FB-2026]`

| Income band | Cumulative upper bound | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| First 375,000 | 375,000 | 0% |
| Next 300,000 | 675,000 | 10% |
| Next 400,000 | 1,075,000 | 15% |
| Next 500,000 | 1,575,000 | 20% |
| Next 2,000,000 | 3,575,000 | 25% |
| Balance | — | 30% |

> The **5% bracket was abolished for AY 2026-27** (it existed in AY 2025-26); the schedule
> now starts at 10%. `[FB-2026]` `[TaxpertBD]`

The slab widths apply to income **above** the taxpayer's tax-free threshold below.

## Salary exemption (employment head) `[ITA-2023]` (6th Sch., Part A, para 27) `[Ramco]`

Exempt the **lower of** (a) one-third of total salary income, or (b) the annual cap.
- **AY 2026-27 cap: BDT 500,000.**

## Investment tax rebate — Section 78 `[ITA-2023]` `[PwC]`

Rebate = **15% of the eligible amount**, where the eligible amount is the **lowest** of:
1. **3% of total income** (excluding exempt income, income at reduced rates, partnership/AOP
   profit shares, and final-tax income),
2. **15% of total allowable investment** (6th Schedule, Part 3),
3. **BDT 1,000,000** (cap; reached at ≈ BDT 6,666,667 of investment).

No rebate if the return is filed after Tax Day (`filed_late=True`).

Eligible investments include: government savings certificates (sanchayapatra), treasury
bonds/securities; recognised provident fund, approved pension & gratuity funds; life
insurance premiums; listed shares/mutual funds/debentures; approved donations; DPS.

- **DPS cap commonly BDT 120,000/yr — ⚠️ unverified; confirm against the gazetted Act.**
- Discard any "BDT 150,000 cap / Tk 1.5 crore investment" figures — those are pre-2023
  ordinance rules and are **wrong** under ITA 2023.

## Minimum tax `[FB-2026]` `[Ramco]`

- **Flat BDT 5,000** for anyone whose income exceeds the tax-free threshold, regardless of
  location. **BDT 1,000** for new taxpayers. Area-based minimum tax and the minimum
  surcharge are abolished from this year.
- **Gross-receipts minimum tax:** an individual with gross receipts > **BDT 40,000,000**
  pays the higher of regular tax, certain WHTs, and **0.25% of gross receipts**.

If tax after rebate is below the minimum, the minimum applies.

## Net-wealth surcharge `[DailyStar]`

Levied on the **income tax payable** (not on assets), based on net wealth disclosed in IT-10B.

| Net wealth | Surcharge (% of income tax) |
|---|---|
| ≤ 4 crore (40,000,000) | 0% |
| > 4cr to 10cr — **also** triggered by owning >1 car OR >8,000 sq ft residential property | 10% |
| > 10cr to 20cr | 20% |
| > 20cr to 50cr | 30% |
| > 50cr | 35% |

A separate **environmental surcharge** applies to multiple-vehicle owners at
registration/fitness renewal; **EVs exempt from AY 2026-27.** `[FB-2026]`

## Other heads & rules `[ITA-2023]` `[PwC]`

- **Seven heads of income:** Employment, Rent, Agriculture, Business, Capital gains,
  Financial assets (interest/dividend), Other sources.
- **House property:** annual rental value less repair allowance — **25% non-commercial /
  30% commercial** — plus municipal/holding tax, land revenue, insurance, housing-loan interest.
- **Capital gains:** generally **15%** (holding-period and listed-security specifics apply).
- **Dividends:** WHT 10% for a resident individual with e-TIN (15% without), 30% non-resident;
  taxed at slab rates; dividend WHT is **minimum tax and non-refundable**.
- **Residency:** resident if ≥ 183 days in the income year, OR ≥ 90 days in the income year
  AND ≥ 365 days across the preceding four years. Residents taxed on worldwide income;
  non-residents on Bangladesh-source income. **Non-resident non-citizens: flat 30%.**
  Non-resident Bangladeshis: ordinary slab rates.
- **Agriculture:** receipts less cultivation expenses. Deemed-cost method where no books:
  commonly **60% of market value treated as cost → 40% income — ⚠️ unverified %.** A special
  additional exemption for solely-agricultural individuals of **~BDT 200,000 — ⚠️ unverified
  amount.** Confirm both against the gazetted Act. *(Not computed by `tax_calc.py`.)*
